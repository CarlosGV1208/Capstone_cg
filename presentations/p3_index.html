Executive Summary

The project's main objective is to develop a data science framework that identifies the economic indicators, institutional conditions, and policy factors that enable or constrain industrial upgrading in resource-rich emerging markets.

Support for Moody's sovereign credit risk assessment:

  • Using machine learning techniques to identify cross-country patterns associated with successful value-added industry development.
  • Conducting in-depth case studies to validate and contextualise quantitative findings.
  • Deriving actionable policy lessons that illuminate sovereign credit risk drivers.

Deliverables: Comprehensive research report (LSE), insights report (Moody's), and presentation for Moody's Insights.

LSE

1. Client Brief

1.1 Primary Questions

  • How successful have emerging market economies been at developing value-added industries from natural resource extraction?
  • What factors explain why some countries have been more successful than others?

1.2 Secondary Questions

  • What policy levers and institutional conditions support or hinder the transition?
  • How sustainable are these shifts (fiscal, employment, export)?
  • How have they supported or hindered credit quality?

1.3 Methodologies

  • Quantitative: Cross-country analysis.
  • Qualitative: Case studies (3-5 EMs with different trajectories).
Planning

Topic and Data Overview / Methodology Plan

  1. Topic and Data Overview: Literature review, Growth Diagnostics framework.
  2. Clustering: Unsupervised learning to group EMEs based on industrial development trajectories.
  3. Quantitative Dual-approach: Correlation Analysis, OLS, and ML Methods (Random Forest, Lasso, Ridge, Elastic Net).
  4. Qualitative Case Studies: Paired case studies (successful vs unsuccessful).
  5. Research Implications: Linking industrial modernization patterns with credit risk factors.

Value Added Indicators (2015-2024)

  • Identify indicators to measure value added generated by each country.
  • Classify resource-intensive countries as successful or unsuccessful.
  • Comparative quantitative analysis with previous years (back to 1990) to identify predictors.
  • Use case studies to verify robustness.

Dual-approach: Econometrics & ML

1. Literature Review

Growth Diagnostics framework to identify binding constraints.

2. Exploratory Analysis

Correlation Analysis and OLS to identify predictors of Economic Complexity.

3. Final Models & Results

Quantify how variables jointly explain differences between "successful" and "unsuccessful" EMEs.

4. Synthesis & Interpretation

Focus on interpretability and predictive power (LASSO/Random Forest).

Research Report Deliverables

1. Research Report

Comprehensive long-form report: methodology, findings, policy implications.

2. Moody's Report

Shorter Insight Report focused on key results and credit risk applications.

3. Presentation

Deck for internal and external audiences.
