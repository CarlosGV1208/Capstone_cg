The Economic Complexity Index (ECI)

What is ECI?

Definition: A measure developed by the Harvard Growth Lab to capture the degree of "know-how" accumulated by a country.

It helps track the transition from low complexity activities (e.g., subsistence farming, oil extraction) towards high complexity activities (electronics, machinery, and chemicals).

How it works: Based on trade data using two key matrix measures: Diversity (number of products exported) and Ubiquity (number of countries exporting those products).

Why did we choose ECI?

  • Better Coverage: Traditional value-added measures (e.g., WBI, TiVA) often have poor data coverage for developing countries. ECI covers a wider range.
  • Granularity: The measure has strong theoretical foundations in the "product space," allowing us to identify specific strengths and weaknesses of a country at a detailed product level.

Theoretical Background:

  • Structural transformation: Economic Complexity (Hidalgo & Hausmann, 2009) + Growth Diagnostics.
  • Institutions: Governance quality as a shaper of development (Acemoglu & Robinson).
  • Resource Management: Conditions for successful rent management (van der Ploeg).

Model 3: With Lagged ECI

$$ \begin{aligned} \log(ECI_{it}) = \beta_0 &+ \beta_1 \log(HCI_{it}) + \beta_2 \log(GFCF_{it}) + \beta_3 \log(Production_{it}) \\ &+ \beta_4 PoliticalStability_{it} + \beta_5 RuleOfLaw_{it} + \beta_6 Trade_{it} \\ &+ \beta_7 [\log(HCI_{it}) \times \log(Production_{it})] \\ &+ \beta_8 [\log(GFCF_{it}) \times \log(Production_{it})] \\ &+ \beta_9 \log(ECI_{i,t-1}) \\ &+ \epsilon_{it} \end{aligned} $$

Note: Includes lagged dependent variable to capture persistence in economic complexity. Standard errors clustered by country.

Variable Definitions
  • ECI: Economic Complexity Index for country i at time t
  • HCI: Human Capital Index
  • GFCF: Gross Fixed Capital Formation (% of GDP)
  • Prod: Total Mineral production value pc
  • Political Stability: Political Stability and Absence of Violence index
  • Rule of Law: Rule of Law index
  • Trade: Trade openness (% of GDP)
Estimation Details
  • Method: Ordinary Least Squares (OLS)
  • Errors: Clustered by country
  • Sample: Resource-rich countries (mineral prod ‚â• $10 pc)
  • Interactions: Mean-centered to reduce multicollinearity

Section 6

Key Findings: Econometrics vs. ML

Comparing the structural inference of Panel Data with the predictive power of Machine Learning.

1. Convergent Findings & Core Drivers

Both highlight the importance of Rule of Law and Political Stability, reinforcing the academic literature on institutions. Additionally, Human Capital (HCI) proves critical in both approaches.

Notable Discovery: The HCI √ó Production interaction is not only statistically significant in econometrics but also holds higher predictive power in ML models than traditional macro variables such as inflation, conflict, or interest rates.

Econometric Insight

Literature Context:

We tested key channels suggested by literature regarding why resource-rich countries struggle to grow: Institutional Quality (Acemoglu & Robinson), Productive Investment (Venables), Macro Uncertainty (van der Ploeg), and Crowding-out effects (Auty, Sachs & Werner).

  • Resource Curse: We reject the hypothesis. When controlling for fixed effects, resources do not inherently drag growth.
  • Solow Growth Model: Validated. Human Capital Index and Gross Fixed Capital Formation are positive and robustly significant.
  • Interaction Effect: The HCI √ó Production term is positive and significant, implying that returns to human capital are compounded for greater extractors of natural resources.

Data Science Insight

Predictive Ordering & Expanded Channels:

The ML approach allowed us to evaluate channels often overlooked in standard econometrics, such as infrastructure quality, IMF fiscal discipline, and lending rates.

  • Key Predictors: Results highlight the importance of functioning capital markets (access to credit), fiscal discipline, and political stability.
  • Dominant Factors: Human Capital Index and its interaction with production consistently rank as top features.
