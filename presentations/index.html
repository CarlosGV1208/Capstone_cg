Section 4

Econometrics Approach

Theoretical Background:

  • Structural transformation: Economic Complexity (Hidalgo & Hausmann, 2009) + Growth Diagnostics.
  • Institutions: Governance quality as a shaper of development (Acemoglu & Robinson).
  • Resource Management: Conditions for successful rent management (van der Ploeg).

Model 1: Panel Data Fixed Effects

$$ ECI_{it} = \alpha + \beta_1 NR_{it} + \beta_2 Cap_{it} + \beta_3 Trade_{it} + \beta_4 Inst_{it} + FE_{i} + \epsilon_{it} $$

Model 2: Interactions (No Lags)

$$ \begin{aligned} \log(ECI_{it}) = \beta_0 &+ \beta_1 \log(HCI_{it}) + \beta_2 \log(GFCF_{it}) + \beta_3 \log(Prod_{it}) \\ &+ \beta_4 PolStab_{it} + \beta_5 RuleLaw_{it} + \beta_6 Trade_{it} \\ &+ \beta_7 [\log(HCI_{it}) \times \log(Prod_{it})] \\ &+ \beta_8 [\log(GFCF_{it}) \times \log(Prod_{it})] + \epsilon_{it} \end{aligned} $$

Note: Interaction terms are mean-centered. Standard errors clustered by country.

Model 3: With Lagged ECI (Persistence)

$$ \begin{aligned} \log(ECI_{it}) = \dots &+ \beta_7 [\log(HCI_{it}) \times \log(Prod_{it})] \\ &+ \beta_8 [\log(GFCF_{it}) \times \log(Prod_{it})] \\ &+ \mathbf{\beta_9 \log(ECI_{i,t-1})} + \epsilon_{it} \end{aligned} $$

Includes lagged dependent variable to capture persistence in economic complexity.

Model 4: Fully Lagged Dynamic Panel

$$ \begin{aligned} \log(ECI_{it}) = \beta_0 &+ \beta_1 \log(HCI_{i,t-1}) + \beta_2 \log(GFCF_{i,t-1}) + \beta_3 \log(Prod_{i,t-1}) \\ &+ \beta_4 PolStab_{i,t-1} + \beta_5 RuleLaw_{i,t-1} + \beta_6 Trade_{i,t-1} \\ &+ \beta_7 [\dots Interaction_{t-1} \dots] \\ &+ \beta_9 \log(ECI_{i,t-1}) + \epsilon_{it} \end{aligned} $$

All explanatory variables are lagged one period (t-1) to address endogeneity and reverse causality.

Variable Definitions
  • ECI: Economic Complexity Index
  • HCI: Human Capital Index
  • GFCF: Gross Fixed Capital Formation
  • Prod: Mineral production value pc
  • PolStab / RuleLaw: Institutional Indices
Estimation Details
  • Method: Ordinary Least Squares (OLS)
  • Errors: Clustered by country
  • Sample: Resource-rich countries (mineral prod ‚â• $10 pc)
  • Interactions: Mean-centered to reduce multicollinearity
